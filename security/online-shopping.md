# Safe Online Shopping Practices

Stay safe while shopping online and protect your financial information.

## Before You Shop

### Verify Website Security
- Look for HTTPS (padlock icon)
- Check domain spelling carefully
- Read reviews and ratings
- Verify business legitimacy
- Check for contact information

### Research the Seller
- Search for company reviews
- Check Better Business Bureau
- Look for social media presence
- Verify physical address
- Read return policies

## During Checkout

### Payment Security
1. Use credit cards (better fraud protection)
2. Consider PayPal or digital wallets
3. Never use debit cards online
4. Use virtual card numbers
5. Enable transaction alerts

### Information to Provide
- ✓ Name and shipping address
- ✓ Email and phone
- ✓ Payment details (secure sites only)
- ✗ Social Security Number
- ✗ More info than necessary

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Deals too good to be true
- Poor website design
- No HTTPS/security certificate
- Requests for unusual payment methods
- Pressure to buy immediately
- Spelling and grammar errors
- No return policy
- No customer service contact

## Safe Shopping Tips

1. **Shop from known retailers**
2. **Use separate email for shopping**
3. **Create unique passwords**
4. **Save confirmation emails**
5. **Monitor bank statements**
6. **Use guest checkout when possible**
7. **Clear browser cache after shopping**

## Mobile Shopping Security

- Download official apps only
- Keep apps updated
- Use secure Wi-Fi (avoid public)
- Enable app security features
- Review app permissions
- Use device lock screen

## After Purchase

- Save order confirmations
- Track shipments
- Review charges on statements
- Check for unauthorized transactions
- Keep receipts for returns
- Monitor credit report

## Payment Methods Ranked

**Most Secure:**
1. Credit cards (fraud protection)
2. PayPal/digital wallets
3. Virtual card numbers
4. Buy now, pay later (verified services)

**Least Secure:**
1. Wire transfers
2. Gift cards
3. Cryptocurrency (non-refundable)
4. Direct bank transfers

## Black Friday/Cyber Monday Tips

- Research prices beforehand
- Beware of fake deal sites
- Use price tracking tools
- Don't rush decisions
- Verify discount authenticity
- Check return windows

## What to Do If Scammed

1. Contact your bank immediately
2. Dispute the charge
3. Report to FTC (ftc.gov/complaint)
4. Document everything
5. Change passwords
6. Monitor credit report
7. Report to local authorities

Remember: If a deal seems too good to be true, it probably is. Always prioritize security over savings.
